# Proposal D — "Forest" ★ personal direction

> **Philosophy.** Warm everywhere, green at heart. A Flexoki ink-on-paper light
> mode and a Gruvbox warm-dark night mode, tied together by a single **forest
> green** signature re-lit per mode. Built from three references — _All About
> Lily Chou-Chou_ (hazy, melancholic green), Wong Kar-Wai (nostalgic, film-grain
> warmth), and the Everforest/Gruvbox/Flexoki family (comfort over punch). Where
> Proposal C split the difference (warm light, _cool_ dark) and kept Journey's
> inherited purple, D commits: one temperature (warm), one identity (green),
> chosen — not inherited.

This is the deliberately personal direction. Where C was "the safe default,"
D is "the one that looks like me." It supersedes C as the intended baseline
**pending typography + spacing sign-off** (this proposal covers color only).

All numbers computed by `tools/color.py` (now with `oklch`/`fit`). The accent and
all semantics were generated by fitting OKLCH lightness to a contrast target
against the real grounds — regenerate any value with
`python3 tools/color.py fit <C> <H> <bg> <target> <light|dark>`.

### The signature — one green, re-lit

The defining move (same idea as C's purple, now committed to green): the forest
hue is held at **150°** across both modes; only lightness changes.

| Mode  | Hex       | OKLCH                   | Character                             |
| ----- | --------- | ----------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Light | `#2d6e3f` | `oklch(48.3% 0.10 150)` | deep forest ink on paper              |
| Dark  | `#7abb87` | `oklch(73.4% 0.10 150)` | soft spring green, glows on warm dark |

Hue 150 is the readable edge of "warm green" — the warmest true green that still
clears AA on light paper before it muddies into olive (see
`research/colorscheme-analysis.md`, cross-scheme lesson on yellow-green
luminance). Green is also the natural foil to the warm brown grounds, which is
exactly why it pops — the Gruvbox move.

**The green clash, resolved.** Because the brand is green, `success` can't also be
plain green or the two blur. `success` is therefore a deliberately distinct
**yellow-lime** (hue ~124°, higher chroma) — clearly "it worked" vs. the deeper
forest "this is a link."

### Honest trade-offs

- **+** Genuinely personal: a chosen green identity and a single warm temperature, drawn from references that mean something to you — not a synthesis-by-committee.
- **+** Both grounds are battle-tested comfort palettes (Flexoki light, Gruvbox dark) you already know you can read for hours.
- **+** Green-on-warm is a high-harmony pairing; the accent pops without shouting.
- **−** Green-as-brand forces `success` into a less-obvious yellow-lime; status-green is no longer the "default" green. Lean on icons for status.
- **−** Yellow-green is the structurally hardest hue on light — the forest accent sits at a fairly dark `L 48%` on paper. It reads as _deep_ green, not vivid. (That darkness is the price of AA; it's a feature for prose.)
- **−** Warm-everywhere drops the per-mode temperature contrast C offered; if you ever want a "cool night," this isn't it.
- **−** Light `muted`/semantics go AA-large on `surface`/`overlay` (same structural limit as every scheme analysed). Keep secondary text on `bg`.
