Measure / line length (65ch)

Typography is the craft of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and appealing. A comfortable measure keeps lines between roughly 45 and 75 characters so the eye can return to the start of the next line without losing its place. This paragraph is capped at --measure (65ch) to demonstrate the ideal reading width.

The Art of Reading on Screens

Good typography is invisible. When a reader glides through a paragraph without friction, the type is doing its job. This article previews how headings, body text, lists, links, and quotes render with the current tokens.

Why measure matters

Lines that run too long tire the eye; lines too short break rhythm. Aim for a measure of 45–75 characters per line.
